## 3. Assumptions

- NRC's fully-free model is Nike's brand investment, not replicable for an indie app — this sets user expectations but not pricing precedent.
- Runna's $120/year pricing survived the Strava acquisition and re-validation, suggesting the market has priced it correctly.
- Teal's aggressive free tier (full resume downloadable free) means job seekers expect to export at least once without paying.
- Jobscan's high price ($30-50/month) works because ATS scanning is a narrow, high-urgency use case, not general job search.

---

## 4. Insights

**RunSmart:**
- The market has converged on ~$10/month annual ($80-120/year) for AI coaching apps.
- The free baseline set by Nike Run Club means basic GPS tracking + a first AI plan must be free, or the app feels extractive.
- What people pay for: adaptation over time (plans that adjust), wearable depth (coach reads your body), and specialized goals (race prep). These are exactly the features that require sustained AI and data — the value is cumulative, not one-time.
- Freemium-to-paid conversion in North America for fitness apps: ~22.4% (source: running apps market report, medium confidence).
- Annual plans at $80-120/year are the dominant format — monthly is a fallback, not the primary.

**Resumely:**
- Market pricing is wide: $8-30/month depending on billing cadence.
- The activation moment (first improved resume + export) is universally in the free tier across all competitors — Teal, Rezi, and Kickresume all let you see a better resume before charging.
- The paywall line is consistently at the AI depth and repetition: a second job application, a second optimize pass, ATS checker.
- Jobscan is the only player that gates the first meaningful action (scan #6), and it is widely criticized for it — confirms the WP-2 principle that gating the activation moment kills retention.
- Rezi's lifetime plan ($149 one-time) is notable: job seekers are often in a sprint (3-month job search), not a marathon — they may not want a recurring subscription.
- Weekly plans ($9/week on Teal) signal high willingness to pay during active job search.

---

## 5. Opportunities

1. **RunSmart paywall line is validated**: Adaptive week 2+ plans are gated by the closest direct competitor (Runna). This is the right cut.
2. **Resumely weekly option**: Teal's $9/week tier captures high-urgency job seekers. A weekly plan or short burst option (e.g., 30-day pass) may outperform monthly for Resumely's audience.
3. **Resumely lifetime option**: Rezi's $149 lifetime is popular. Could offer a "job search sprint" one-time unlock as an upsell path if subscription conversion is weak (revisit only if data supports).
4. **RunSmart annual-first framing**: Runna leads with annual ($120/year) and Strava does too ($80/year). Annual should be the primary CTA, not monthly.

---

## 6. Risks

- Nike Run Club's free model raises user expectations. If RunSmart's free tier is too thin, early reviews will say "feels like a trial."
- If Resumely gates too aggressively (fewer than 1 full optimize + export free), Teal and Rezi will win the comparison.
- Jobscan's pricing backlash shows that high price + restricted free tier = churn and bad press.
- Rezi's lifetime option creates a revenue concentration risk (one-time vs recurring) — only worth considering if subscription conversion is persistently low.

---

## 7. Recommendations (ranked)

1. **Lock the RunSmart paywall at adaptive week 2+ plans and wearable depth** — validated by Runna, aligns with WP-2 candidate.
2. **Lock the Resumely paywall at the second optimize / second export** — aligns with Teal and Rezi, keeps activation fully free.
3. **Price anchor in the $8-12/month annual range for RunSmart** — fill in exact price after activation data (EXD-009).
4. **Consider weekly plan for Resumely** ($9-13/week) as the high-urgency tier — add to StoreKit product ID draft.
5. **Annual plan is the primary CTA for RunSmart; monthly is the fallback** — reverse for Resumely (job seekers prefer shorter commitment).
