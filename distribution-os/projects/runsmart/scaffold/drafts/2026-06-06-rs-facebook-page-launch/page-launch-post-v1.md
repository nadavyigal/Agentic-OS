# Facebook Page Post: RunSmart Launch

## Hypothesis

A concise Facebook page launch post that leads with the runner outcome will earn
more qualified App Store clicks than a feature-list announcement because it is
easy to understand, easy to share, and tied to the approval moment.

## Target Audience

Beginner and returning runners whose training plans break when life, missed
sessions, soreness, or recovery changes the week.

## Draft

RunSmart is now available on the App Store.

It is an adaptive running coach for beginners and returning runners who need a
clear answer to one question: what should I do today?

Build a plan around your goal, log the runs you actually complete, and let
RunSmart help adjust the week when training does not go perfectly.

If you are starting again, training around a busy schedule, or tired of static
plans that ignore real life, you can try it here:

[APP STORE LINK]

## Screenshot Note

Use one approved App Store screenshot or a simple Today screen capture that shows
the next recommended run and plan context. Do not use a screenshot that exposes
private health data, location details, or unsupported integrations.

## Publish Gate

- Do not publish until App Store Connect shows RunSmart is approved and available.
- Replace `[APP STORE LINK]` with the verified public listing URL.
- Founder must change `approved: no` to `approved: yes - YYYY-MM-DD`.
- Re-read the adaptive plan, Garmin, Apple Health, GPS, and readiness language
  against the approved build before publishing.

## Measurement

Review seven days after publishing:

- Facebook reach
- Reactions, comments, and shares
- App Store link clicks, if tracked
- App Store product page views and installs from the same window, if available
- Any relevant replies from runners

## Self-Critique

- [x] The post is shorter and more direct than A1.
- [x] The post leads with the outcome, not a feature list.
- [x] The CTA is explicit and appears once.
- [x] No performance, injury-prevention, medical, customer-result, or adoption
  claim is made.
- [x] Publishing remains blocked on founder approval and a verified App Store URL.
