# LinkedIn Post: RunSmart Launch

## Hypothesis

A specific founder story about the limits of fixed running plans will earn more
qualified interest than feature-led launch copy because it begins with a problem
beginner and returning runners recognize.

## Target Audience

Beginner and returning runners whose training plans break when life, recovery,
or missed sessions change the week.

## Draft

Most running plans work well until you miss Tuesday.

That was the problem I kept running into as a runner: the plan had no idea
whether I slept badly, felt sore, missed a session, or had a better week than
expected. It simply kept moving.

I built RunSmart because I wanted something closer to a calm coach than a fixed
calendar.

RunSmart gives you a clear answer about what to do today, builds a plan around
your goal, and adjusts it based on the runs you actually complete. It can bring
in Garmin and Apple Health activity data, then turn that context into a
recommendation instead of another dashboard full of numbers.

The point is not to push harder every day. It is to know when to run, when to
back off, and why.

Building it has forced me to rethink what useful AI in fitness should feel like:
less hype, fewer charts, and one practical next step that respects the runner in
front of it.

RunSmart is now available on the App Store.

If you are starting again, training around a busy life, or tired of plans that
assume every week goes perfectly, you can try it here:
