# Distribution Context

The shared context any distribution agent loads first. Keep this short and current.

## Founder

Solo founder. Mac-based. Builds both products in parallel. Not a content creator; will not manage many social channels manually. Prefers shipping over performing. Approves every external publish.

## Two Products

### RunSmart

- AI running coach. **Native iOS app is the primary distribution surface**; web is the supporting funnel.
- Audience: beginner to intermediate runners. Garmin, Apple Health, Strava users. Goal-oriented (5K, 10K, half, marathon) and habit-oriented (consistency, recovery, plan adherence).
- Core wedge: adaptive AI plan generation, post-run debrief, readiness check, race strategy. Free running route benchmarks and challenge-based campaigns are warm wedges.
- Distribution surfaces (priority order):
  1. App Store Optimization
  2. Product-led landing pages (AI running coach, beginner runner, Garmin running analysis, adaptive plans)
  3. LinkedIn founder updates
  4. Running SEO content (long tail: "adaptive marathon plan", "Garmin running coach", "beginner 5k plan AI")
  5. Runna comparison page
  6. Garmin / Strava integration content
  7. Beginner runner challenges
  8. Partnerships with coaches, clubs, podcasts
  9. Lifecycle email and onboarding
  10. Community research (Reddit r/running, r/running coaching) — observation only, no spam
- Avoid: paid ads as a default. No mass cold outreach. No challenges that fake community.

### ResumeBuilder AI

- AI resume optimization and resume building. **iOS app is the primary distribution surface.** Web exists but acts as a feeder to iOS install.
- Audience: active job seekers, career switchers, and people improving an existing resume. English-first, Hebrew secondary.
- Core wedge: ATS-aware optimization, job-tailored output, polished templates, confidence-building edits — delivered as a mobile-first workspace.
- Distribution surfaces (priority order):
  1. App Store Optimization
  2. Web landing pages that funnel to App Store install (resume templates, ATS terms, AI resume tailoring)
  3. Free ATS / resume scoring tool (web; result page CTAs to iOS app)
  4. Directory submissions (AI tools, AI apps, career tools, resume tool directories — include App Store URL)
  5. Programmatic SEO (resume examples, role / industry pages — primary CTA is App Store install)
  6. Lifecycle email (signup, activation, return, paid)
  7. Career coach and HR partnerships
  8. LinkedIn job seeker content
  9. Conversion optimization on the editor, signup, and pricing surfaces
  10. Hebrew market localization (separate landing experience plus authored Hebrew App Store metadata)
- Avoid: paid ads as a default. No spammy outreach to job seekers. No fake testimonials. No web SEO that converts only to web signup when iOS install is the goal.

## Constraints

- No external automation platforms (n8n, Zapier, Make, Activepieces). All "automation" is Claude Code or Codex executing markdown workflows.
- No new dependencies without founder approval.
- No secrets in code or in this folder.
- Google Drive is the document store. Notion is the planning store. Git is the operating system. Project repos are the product source of truth.
- Free / low-cost distribution first. Paid ads only when an organic channel proves repeatably and a paid amplification has a clear ROI hypothesis.
- One product gets weekly focus at a time. The other gets maintenance-only that week.

## Founder Preferences

- Practical product-led distribution
- SEO, LinkedIn, partnerships, directories, free tools, lifecycle email
- Structured weekly execution, not daily scramble
- One repeatable LinkedIn cadence (founder updates) is fine; no daily Twitter
- Partnerships with real people, with real personal context, not blasts
- Quality over volume on every channel

## Definitions Used Across This OS

- **Channel**: a distribution surface (e.g., ASO, programmatic SEO, LinkedIn founder updates)
- **Asset**: a concrete thing the agent produces (SEO brief, post draft, directory submission, email draft)
- **Experiment**: a hypothesis tested via a channel with a metric and a time box
- **Campaign**: a coordinated set of assets across one or more channels with a single goal
- **Approved**: founder has signed off in writing (Notion, file header, or chat) and the asset is cleared to publish
