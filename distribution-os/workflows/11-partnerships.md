# Partnerships Workflow

Slow channel, high conviction. The founder's preferred outreach surface (real relationships, not blasts).

## Inputs

- Partner profile (who would be valuable)
- Existing relationships
- Product positioning

## Steps

### 1. Load skill

`marketingskills/skills/co-marketing/SKILL.md`. Optionally `sales-enablement` only when partnerships scale enough to need recurring materials.

### 2. Define the partner profile per product

RunSmart partners:

- Running coaches with a small but engaged audience (newsletter, podcast, club)
- Independent running clubs
- Niche running podcasts
- Garmin / Strava ecosystem creators
- Adjacent fitness creators (sparingly)

ResumeBuilder partners:

- Career coaches with a job-seeker audience
- HR consultants
- University career centers
- Bootcamp career services
- Productivity / career newsletters

### 3. Build the prospect list (slow)

Maximum 10 to 20 at any time. For each:

- Name and role
- Audience size and quality
- Why this partner fits this product
- Existing relationship (1 to 5)
- Realistic ask
- Realistic offer

Save to Drive `02 Market Research/{product}/partnerships/prospects.md`.

### 4. Draft a personal outreach (one per partner, not a template)

Each outreach must:

- Reference something real about the partner (not the product they would be marketing)
- State a small, specific ask
- Offer something the partner actually wants (early access, a tailored asset, a guest spot, real reciprocity)
- Be under 150 words

No mail merge. No campaign tooling. Each outreach is personal.

### 5. Track

- Sent / replied / met / collaborated / dormant
- A row in `experiment-log.md` per partnership track

### 6. Joint asset patterns

Once a partnership is active, common joint assets:

- Guest post or newsletter swap
- Joint webinar / Q&A (RunSmart specifically: a "running questions answered" with a coach)
- Coupon / referral specific to the partner
- Co-branded landing page (only if the partnership is real and ongoing)

Produce assets via the relevant skill (`copywriting`, `emails`, `cro`).

### 7. Anti-patterns

- Cold blast with no personal context
- Tit-for-tat list of links
- Affiliate deals before there is a product to recommend
- Partnerships that require the founder to be on stage weekly

### 8. Cadence

Outreach is bursty: 5 to 10 sends per quarter, not weekly. Activity in this channel does not need to fill a weekly slot.
