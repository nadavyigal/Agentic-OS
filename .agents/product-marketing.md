# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-30*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** RunSmart is the AI running coach for beginners and returning runners who need safe daily adaptation, not just faster race plans.
**What it does:** RunSmart gives runners a daily training answer, adapts plans when real life gets in the way, explains post-run performance in plain language, and uses Garmin / Apple Health data where available to turn activity history into coaching decisions.
**Product category:** AI running coach, training plan app, coach-on-demand, run analysis tool.
**Product type:** Native iOS app.
**Business model:** Free at launch. Paid tier is not live and should not be claimed until approved.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Not applicable, consumer fitness app.
**Decision-makers:** Individual runners.
**Primary use case:** A beginner, returning, or intermediate runner wants a plan that adapts to their body and week instead of a rigid schedule.
**Jobs to be done:**
- When I wake up, I want to know if I should run and at what effort, in plain language, so I can make a confident decision quickly.
- When my plan feels wrong for my body today, I want the app to explain or adapt it so I do not have to choose between the plan and my instincts.
- When I finish a workout, I want to see how it contributed to progress so I feel momentum building.
- When I am tired or sore, I want the app to hold me back with a reason I trust.
**Use cases:**
- First 5K or 10K plan.
- Returning after a break or injury scare.
- Training around a busy week.
- Understanding Garmin or Apple Health data without becoming a dashboard analyst.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Static running plans do not respond to fatigue, missed sessions, life changes, or how the runner actually feels.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- PDF and template plans are rigid.
- Generic fitness apps show activity, but do not coach the decision.
- Human coaches are expensive and overkill for many beginners.
- Race-plan apps can feel too intense for runners whose real goal is consistency.
**What it costs them:** Missed weeks, injury anxiety, abandoned plans, and second-guessing every run.
**Emotional tension:** The user wants to improve, but does not fully trust either their plan or their impulse to push through.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Runna, Garmin Coach, Nike Run Club, Strava training plans, AI running apps.
**Secondary:** Free online plans, running calculators, watch dashboards.
**Indirect:** Human coaches, personal spreadsheets, ChatGPT prompts, doing nothing.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Adaptive plan generation that responds to actual sessions.
- Plain-language post-run debrief.
- Readiness and recovery framing with safety-first defaults.
- Race strategy at the right moments.
- Garmin / Apple Health data translated into decisions, not just dashboards.
- Conversational coaching that explains the "why" behind recommendations.
**How we do it differently:** RunSmart frames itself as a daily coaching layer, not a fixed race calendar or generic AI wrapper.
**Why that's better:** The user gets a credible answer for today, not a plan they have to reinterpret when life changes.
**Why customers choose us:** They want consistency and confidence more than elite-intensity training.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I can get a free plan online." | Free plans can tell you what a typical week looks like. RunSmart helps decide what makes sense for your body today. |
| "I already have Garmin / Apple Health." | Those tools collect useful signals. RunSmart turns those signals into coaching language and training decisions. |
| "I am not a serious runner." | RunSmart is built especially for beginners and returning runners who want consistency without pressure. |

**Anti-persona:** Elite or sub-elite runners looking for maximal performance engineering, runners who want a marketplace human coach, or users who only want passive step tracking.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Rigid plans, injury worries, generic dashboards, missed sessions that derail the week.
**Pull:** A calm daily answer, adaptation, plain-language debriefs, safety confidence.
**Habit:** Continuing with free plans, Garmin defaults, Strava, or no structure.
**Anxiety:** "Will this actually understand my fitness?" and "Will it push me too hard?"

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "I do not know what I should run today."
- "My plan does not fit my week."
- "I have the data, but I do not know what it means."
- "I do not want to get injured again."
**How they describe us:**
- "A coach that reads your body every morning."
- "A plan that adapts when life gets in the way."
**Words to use:** adaptive, readiness, safe, daily answer, plain language, consistency, plan, debrief, progress.
**Words to avoid:** revolutionary, guaranteed results, injury prevention guarantees, elite performance claims, generic "AI-powered" as the main hook.
**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Readiness | A daily signal for whether and how hard to run |
| Debrief | Plain-language post-run explanation |
| Adaptive plan | Training plan that changes based on actual sessions and context |
