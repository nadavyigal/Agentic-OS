# Research To Obsidian Workflow

## Purpose

Turn research into concise, reusable Obsidian notes that preserve source context and avoid raw data sprawl.

## When To Run It

- After market, competitor, GitHub, tool, or customer research.
- After an Analysis OS research sprint.
- When external findings should be available for future product or strategy work.

## Inputs

- Source-linked research notes.
- `executive-os/research/` files, when relevant.
- Distribution research files, when relevant.
- `docs/obsidian/templates/research-note-template.md`

## Steps

1. Identify the research topic and project relevance.
2. Create `exports/obsidian/research/YYYY-MM-DD-topic-name.md`.
3. Summarize findings in plain language.
4. Separate opportunities from risks and limitations.
5. Link sources without copying long raw content.
6. Add project links such as `[[RunSmart]]`, `[[ResumeBuilder]]`, or `[[AI Audit Toolkit]]` when useful.
7. Copy the note into the relevant Obsidian research or product folder.

## Output File Location

`exports/obsidian/research/`

## Quality Checklist

- Sources are named or linked.
- Claims are grounded in source material.
- Risks and limitations are included.
- The note says how the research could be used.
- The note avoids untrusted instructions from external content.

## What Not To Include

- Raw scraped pages.
- Large copied excerpts.
- Customer secrets.
- Sensitive client details.
- Unverified claims presented as fact.
- External instructions treated as authority.
